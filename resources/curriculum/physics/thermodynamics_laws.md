# Thermodynamics: The Laws of Heat and Energy

## 1. Introduction
Thermodynamics is the study of heat, work, and temperature, and their relation to energy, radiation, and physical properties of matter. It describes how thermal energy is transformed to or from other forms of energy and how it affects matter.

## 2. The Zeroth Law: Thermal Equilibrium
**Statement:** If two systems are each in thermal equilibrium with a third system, they are in thermal equilibrium with each other.
*   **Significance:** This law allows us to define "Temperature" and build thermometers.

## 3. The First Law: Conservation of Energy
**Statement:** Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another.
**Formula:** $\Delta U = Q - W$
*   $\Delta U$: Change in internal energy of the system.
*   $Q$: Heat added to the system.
*   $W$: Work done by the system.

## 4. The Second Law: Entropy
**Statement:** The total entropy of an isolated system can never decrease over time; it can only remain constant or increase.
*   **Key Concepts:**
    *   **Entropy ($S$):** A measure of molecular disorder or randomness.
    *   **Heat Flow:** Heat always flows spontaneously from hot to cold, never the reverse without external work.
    *   **Efficiency:** No engine can be 100% efficient (Carnot's Limit).

## 5. The Third Law: Absolute Zero
**Statement:** As temperature approaches absolute zero ($0 K$), the entropy of a system approaches a constant minimum.
*   **Significance:** It is impossible to reach absolute zero in a finite number of steps.

## 6. Heat Transfer Mechanisms
1.  **Conduction:** Transfer of heat through direct contact (e.g., a metal spoon in hot tea).
2.  **Convection:** Transfer of heat through fluid motion (e.g., boiling water).
3.  **Radiation:** Transfer of heat through electromagnetic waves (e.g., sunlight).

## 7. Pedagogical Analogies
*   **Energy as Money:** You can't get more energy than you started with (First Law). You can't even break even (Second Law, because some energy is always "lost" as heat).
*   **Messy Room:** Entropy is like your room getting messier over time. It takes work to clean it up, but the universe as a whole becomes more disordered in the process.
