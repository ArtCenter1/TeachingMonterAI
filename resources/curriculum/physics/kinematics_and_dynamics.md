# Kinematics and Dynamics

## Introduction to Kinematics
Kinematics is the branch of mechanics that describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion.

### Core Concepts
1. **Position, Displacement, and Distance**
   - Position ($x$): The location of an object relative to a reference point.
   - Displacement ($\Delta x$): The change in position ($x_f - x_i$). It is a vector quantity.
   - Distance: The total path length traveled. It is a scalar quantity.

2. **Velocity and Speed**
   - Average Velocity: Displacement divided by time ($\Delta x / \Delta t$).
   - Instantaneous Velocity: The velocity at a specific moment.
   - Speed: The rate at which distance is covered (scalar).

3. **Acceleration**
   - Acceleration ($a$): The rate of change of velocity ($\Delta v / \Delta t$).
   - Uniformly Accelerated Motion (UAM): Motion with constant acceleration.

### The Kinematic Equations (for constant acceleration)
1. $v = v_0 + at$
2. $\Delta x = v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2$
3. $v^2 = v_0^2 + 2a\Delta x$
4. $\Delta x = \frac{v + v_0}{2}t$

## Introduction to Dynamics
Dynamics is the study of the forces that cause objects and systems to move.

### Newton's Laws of Motion
1. **First Law (Inertia)**: An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with a constant velocity unless acted upon by a net external force.
2. **Second Law ($F = ma$)**: The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass.
3. **Third Law (Action-Reaction)**: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

### Common Forces
- **Gravity ($W = mg$)**: The force with which the earth pulls on an object.
- **Normal Force ($F_N$)**: The perpendicular contact force exerted by a surface on an object.
- **Friction ($f = \mu F_N$)**: The force that opposes motion between surfaces.
- **Tension ($T$)**: The pulling force transmitted through a string or cable.

### Circular Motion and Centripetal Force
- Centripetal acceleration ($a_c = v^2/r$): Acceleration directed toward the center of a circular path.
- Centripetal force ($F_c = mv^2/r$): The net force causing circular motion.

## Misconceptions in Mechanics
- **Misconception**: Acceleration and velocity are the same thing.
  - **Correction**: Velocity is the rate of change of position; acceleration is the rate of change of velocity.
- **Misconception**: A constant force is needed to keep an object moving.
  - **Correction**: According to Newton's First Law, no net force is needed to maintain constant velocity; force is only needed to *change* velocity.
- **Misconception**: The normal force is always equal to the weight.
  - **Correction**: The normal force depends on the situation (e.g., on an incline or when other vertical forces are present).
