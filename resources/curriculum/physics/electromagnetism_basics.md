# Introduction to Electromagnetism

## 1. What is Electromagnetism?
Electromagnetism is the physical interaction among electric charges, magnetic moments, and the electromagnetic field. It is one of the four fundamental forces of nature.

## 2. Electric Charge and Force
*   **Charge ($q$):** Property of matter (Positive/Negative). Measured in Coulombs ($C$).
*   **Coulomb's Law:** The force between two charges is proportional to the product of the charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.
    $$ F = k \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} $$

## 3. Electric Fields and Potential
*   **Electric Field ($\vec{E}$):** The force per unit charge at a point in space. $\vec{E} = \vec{F}/q$.
*   **Voltage ($V$):** The electric potential difference (energy per unit charge).

## 4. Current, Resistance, and Ohm's Law
*   **Current ($I$):** The rate of flow of charge. $I = dq/dt$.
*   **Resistance ($R$):** The opposition to current flow.
*   **Ohm's Law:** $V = IR$.

## 5. Magnetism
*   **Magnetic Field ($\vec{B}$):** Created by moving charges (currents).
*   **Lorentz Force:** The force on a charge moving through a magnetic field: $\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$.

## 6. Electromagnetism (The Link)
*   **Faraday's Law:** A changing magnetic field induces an electric current in a loop. (The basis of electric generators).
*   **Maxwell's Equations:** The complete set of equations governing all classical electromagnetic phenomena.
