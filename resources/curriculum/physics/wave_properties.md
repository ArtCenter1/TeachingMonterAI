# Wave Properties and Sound

## 1. What is a Wave?
A wave is a disturbance that travels through space and matter, transferring energy from one point to another without transferring matter.

## 2. Types of Waves
*   **Mechanical Waves:** Require a medium to travel (e.g., sound, water waves).
*   **Electromagnetic Waves:** Can travel through a vacuum (e.g., light, radio waves).
*   **Transverse Waves:** Particles move perpendicular to the wave direction (e.g., light).
*   **Longitudinal Waves:** Particles move parallel to the wave direction (e.g., sound).

## 3. Anatomy of a Wave
*   **Crest:** The highest point.
*   **Trough:** The lowest point.
*   **Amplitude ($A$):** Maximum displacement from equilibrium (related to energy/loudness).
*   **Wavelength ($\lambda$):** Distance between consecutive identical points (e.g., crest to crest).
*   **Frequency ($f$):** Number of waves per second (measured in Hertz, $Hz$).
*   **Period ($T$):** Time for one complete wave ($T = 1/f$).

## 4. The Wave Equation
$$ v = f \lambda $$
Where $v$ is the speed of the wave.

## 5. Wave Behaviors
*   **Reflection:** Bouncing off a boundary.
*   **Refraction:** Bending when entering a new medium (change in speed).
*   **Diffraction:** Bending around corners or through openings.
*   **Interference:** Two waves overlapping (Constructive vs. Destructive).
*   **Doppler Effect:** Change in frequency due to relative motion (e.g., a passing siren).

## 6. Pedagogical Tips
*   **Misconception:** Students think waves move matter. Use the "Stadium Wave" analogy: People stand up and sit down (vibration), but they stay in their seats (no matter transfer).
*   **Sound:** Remind students that sound is a pressure wave (longitudinal).
