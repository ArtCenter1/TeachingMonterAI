# Cell Division: Mitosis and Meiosis

## 1. The Importance of Cell Division
Cell division is the process by which a parent cell divides into two or more daughter cells. It is essential for growth, repair, and reproduction.

## 2. The Cell Cycle
The life of a cell consists of:
*   **Interphase:** The longest phase (90%). Cell grows ($G_1$), replicates DNA ($S$), and prepares for division ($G_2$).
*   **M Phase:** The actual division phase.

## 3. Mitosis: Somatic Cell Division
Produces two genetically identical daughter cells (diploid to diploid). Used for growth and tissue repair.

### Phases of Mitosis (PMAT):
1.  **Prophase:** Chromosomes condense, nuclear envelope breaks down.
2.  **Metaphase:** Chromosomes line up in the middle.
3.  **Anaphase:** Sister chromatids are pulled apart to opposite poles.
4.  **Telophase:** Two new nuclei form.
5.  **Cytokinesis:** The cytoplasm divides.

## 4. Meiosis: Gamete Production
Produces four non-identical daughter cells (diploid to haploid). Used for sexual reproduction.

### Key Features:
*   **Two Rounds of Division:** Meiosis I and Meiosis II.
*   **Genetic Variation:** 
    *   **Crossing Over:** Occurs in Prophase I; homologous chromosomes exchange segments.
    *   **Independent Assortment:** Random orientation of homologous pairs in Metaphase I.

## 5. Comparison: Mitosis vs. Meiosis

| Feature | Mitosis | Meiosis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Purpose** | Growth/Repair | Sexual Reproduction |
| **Daughter Cells** | 2 | 4 |
| **Genetic Match** | Identical | Unique |
| **Chromosome #** | Stays Same ($2n \to 2n$) | Reduced by Half ($2n \to n$) |

## 6. Pedagogical Tips
*   **The Sock Analogy:** Use pairs of socks to represent homologous chromosomes. Mitosis is like making a copy of every sock. Meiosis is like splitting the collection to give someone else half.
*   **Hand Mnemonics:** Use fingers to demonstrate the "lining up" and "pulling apart" motions of chromosomes.
*   **Misconception:** Students often confuse "sister chromatids" (identical copies) with "homologous chromosomes" (similar but non-identical pairs from each parent).
