# Evolution: The Mechanisms of Change

## 1. Introduction
Evolution is the process by which the heritable characteristics of biological populations change over successive generations. It is the unifying principle of biology.

## 2. Natural Selection
Proposed by Charles Darwin, this is the primary mechanism of evolution.

### The Four Tenets:
1.  **Variation:** Individuals in a population differ in their traits.
2.  **Heritability:** Some of these traits can be passed to offspring.
3.  **Overproduction:** More offspring are produced than can survive.
4.  **Differential Reproductive Success:** Individuals with traits better suited to their environment are more likely to survive and reproduce ("Survival of the Fittest").

## 3. Other Mechanisms of Evolution
*   **Mutation:** Random changes in DNA; the ultimate source of all genetic variation.
*   **Gene Flow:** Movement of alleles between populations (migration).
*   **Genetic Drift:** Random changes in allele frequencies (especially in small populations, e.g., Bottleneck Effect, Founder Effect).
*   **Sexual Selection:** Selection based on traits that improve mating success (e.g., peacock feathers).

## 4. Evidence for Evolution
*   **Fossil Record:** Showing transition species.
*   **Homologous Structures:** Similar anatomical structures in different species (e.g., human arm vs. whale fin).
*   **Molecular Biology:** Comparing DNA sequences; all life shares a universal genetic code.
*   **Direct Observation:** Antibiotic resistance in bacteria.

## 5. Pedagogical Tips
*   **Misconception: Individuals evolve.** Clarify that populations evolve over generations; individuals just live and die.
*   **Misconception: Evolution is a "ladder" of progress.** Explain that it is a branching bush, and "fitness" just means being well-suited to a *specific* environment at a *specific* time.
*   **Analogy: The Filter.** Evolution is like a sieve. The environment is the filter that decides which "shapes" (traits) get through to the next generation.
