# The Human Circulatory System

## 1. Overview
The circulatory system (or cardiovascular system) is responsible for transporting oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products throughout the body. It consists of the heart, blood vessels, and blood.

## 2. The Heart
The heart is a muscular organ that acts as a dual pump.
*   **Right Side:** Receives deoxygenated blood from the body and pumps it to the lungs (Pulmonary Circulation).
*   **Left Side:** Receives oxygenated blood from the lungs and pumps it to the rest of the body (Systemic Circulation).
*   **Four Chambers:** Right Atrium, Right Ventricle, Left Atrium, Left Ventricle.

## 3. Blood Vessels
*   **Arteries:** Carry blood *away* from the heart. Usually oxygenated (except pulmonary artery). Have thick, elastic walls.
*   **Veins:** Carry blood *toward* the heart. Usually deoxygenated (except pulmonary vein). Have valves to prevent backflow.
*   **Capillaries:** Tiny vessels where gas and nutrient exchange occurs with tissues.

## 4. Components of Blood
*   **Plasma:** Liquid part (mostly water).
*   **Red Blood Cells (Erythrocytes):** Carry oxygen via hemoglobin.
*   **White Blood Cells (Leukocytes):** Immune response.
*   **Platelets:** Blood clotting.

## 5. Blood Pressure
The force exerted by blood against vessel walls.
*   **Systolic:** Pressure when heart contracts.
*   **Diastolic:** Pressure when heart relaxes.

## 6. Pedagogical Analogies
*   **The Mail Delivery System:** Blood vessels are the roads, blood is the delivery trucks, and the heart is the central sorting facility/engine.
*   **The Central Heating System:** Water (blood) is pumped through pipes (vessels) to keep the house (body) warm and functional.

## 7. Common Student Misconceptions
*   **Blue Blood:** Students often think deoxygenated blood is blue because veins look blue through the skin. Clarify that blood is always red (dark red when deoxygenated, bright red when oxygenated).
*   **Heart Shape:** Students think the heart looks like the "valentine" symbol. Show an actual anatomical diagram to show it's more like a clenched fist.
